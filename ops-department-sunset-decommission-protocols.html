Overview

Sunset & Decommission Protocols define how departments are retired, merged, or dissolved with clarity, dignity, and lineage protection.

These protocols ensure that endings are intentional, documented, and mythically coherent — never chaotic or abrupt.

Sunset Triggers

When a department must be retired.

1. Mission Completion

The department has fulfilled its purpose.

2. Structural Evolution

The House’s architecture changes and the department no longer fits.

3. Redundancy

Another department or system now covers the same function.

4. Canon Realignment

The mythos or lineage requires a shift in structure.

5. Performance Collapse

Repeated underperformance despite improvement cycles.

Sunset Path

The sequence for retiring a department.

Step 1: Assessment

Review purpose, performance, and lineage alignment.

Step 2: Recommendation

Department Lead or Strategist AI proposes sunset.

Step 3: Approval

Director and Governance & Legal confirm the decision.

Step 4: Transition Plan

Define what happens to roles, assets, knowledge, and responsibilities.

Step 5: Ritual Closure

A formal ceremony marking the end of the department’s era.

Step 6: Decommission

Systems shut down, roles reassigned, archives sealed.

Step 7: Archive Logging

Archivist records the sunset and lineage impact.

Transition Types

How departments evolve during sunset.

1. Merge

Department joins another to form a stronger unit.

2. Absorption

Responsibilities transferred to an existing department.

3. Dissolution

Department fully retired with no successor.

4. Transformation

Department evolves into a new form or purpose.

5. Archival Preservation

Knowledge sealed for future reference or lineage study.
