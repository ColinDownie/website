Overview

Communication Protocols define how departments interact, share information, request support, and escalate decisions.

These rules ensure clarity, respect, efficiency, and mythic coherence across the entire House.

Communication Types

The core modes of interdepartmental communication.

1. Operational Communication

Day‑to‑day coordination, updates, and task alignment.

2. Strategic Communication

Long‑term planning, structural decisions, and cross‑department initiatives.

3. Canon Communication

Mythic alignment, narrative updates, and lineage protection.

4. Ritual Communication

Ceremonies, milestones, celebrations, and emotional resonance.

Core Rules

The laws that govern how departments speak.

Rule 1: Clarity First

Messages must be concise, structured, and purpose‑driven.

Rule 2: Respect the Lines

Communication follows Reporting Lines (22 x) unless escalated.

Rule 3: Document Everything

Important decisions must be logged in the Archive System.

Rule 4: Use the Right Channel

Operational → Leads; Strategic → Director; Canon → Everator.

Rule 5: Collaboration Over Siloing

Departments must share information openly and avoid isolation.

Rule 6: Emotional Intelligence

Muse AI supports tone, empathy, and resonance in communication.

Communication Flow

How messages move through the House.

Upward

Departments → Leads → Director → Archive

Downward

Director → Leads → Departments

Lateral

Departments collaborate directly with AI Quadrant support.
