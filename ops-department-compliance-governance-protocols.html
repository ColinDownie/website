Overview

Compliance & Governance Protocols define how departments operate within legal, structural, and procedural boundaries.

These protocols ensure consistency, accountability, transparency, and mythic coherence across the House of Ross.

Governance Domains

The areas governed by House law.

1. Legal Compliance

Adherence to external laws, regulations, and obligations.

2. Internal Policy Compliance

Alignment with House rules, standards, and protocols.

3. Canon Governance

Protection of lineage, mythos, and narrative coherence.

4. Operational Governance

Ensuring workflows, systems, and processes follow standards.

5. Cultural Governance

Safeguarding rituals, wellbeing, and emotional architecture.

Compliance Expectations

What every department must uphold.

1. Documentation

All actions must be recorded and traceable.

2. Transparency

Clear communication of decisions, risks, and changes.

3. Accountability

Departments own their actions and outcomes.

4. Alignment

All work must follow House standards and canon.

5. Review Readiness

Departments must be prepared for audits and evaluations.

Enforcement

How compliance is upheld.

1. Governance & Legal

Primary enforcement body; ensures fairness and due process.

2. Department Leads

First line of compliance oversight.

3. Strategist AI

Identifies structural risks and compliance gaps.

4. Everator

Ensures mythic and lineage alignment in governance.

5. Archivist AI

Maintains compliance records and governance history.

6. Director

Final authority on major compliance decisions.
