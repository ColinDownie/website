Overview

Continuity & Contingency Protocols define how departments maintain operations during disruption, transition, or unexpected events.

These protocols ensure stability, resilience, and mythic coherence across the House of Ross — even under pressure.

Continuity Elements

The foundations of uninterrupted operation.

1. Redundant Roles

Backup personnel or AI support for critical functions.

2. Backup Systems

Duplicate tools, templates, and workflows stored securely.

3. Knowledge Preservation

All processes documented in the Archive System.

4. Cross‑Training

Departments trained to support each other during disruption.

5. AI Quadrant Continuity

Strategist, Everator, Muse, and Archivist maintain operational flow.

Contingency Triggers

When contingency plans must activate.

1. System Failure

Tools, platforms, or workflows become unavailable.

2. Personnel Unavailability

Key roles temporarily or permanently unavailable.

3. Structural Disruption

Major changes affecting multiple departments.

4. Canon Emergency

Mythic or lineage contradictions requiring immediate correction.

5. Cultural Shock

Events impacting wellbeing, morale, or emotional architecture.

Contingency Path

The sequence for activating contingency plans.

Step 1: Detection

Department identifies disruption or receives AI Quadrant alert.

Step 2: Stabilisation

Immediate actions to prevent further impact.

Step 3: Activation

Backup roles, systems, and workflows come online.

Step 4: Communication

Clear updates shared across departments and leadership.

Step 5: Resolution

Issue resolved through collaboration and structural support.

Step 6: Archive Logging

Archivist records the event and lessons learned.
