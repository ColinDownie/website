Overview

The Knowledge Management System defines how the House of Ross stores, retrieves, evolves, and protects its collective intelligence.

This system ensures continuity, clarity, accessibility, and mythic coherence across all departments.

Knowledge Types

The core categories of knowledge within the House.

1. Explicit Knowledge

Documents, templates, workflows, archives.

2. Tacit Knowledge

Experience, intuition, creative instincts, emotional architecture.

3. Canon Knowledge

Mythos, lineage, narrative coherence, House laws.

4. Operational Knowledge

Systems, tools, processes, best practices.

5. Cultural Knowledge

Rituals, values, wellbeing practices, House identity.

Storage System

Where knowledge lives.

1. Archive System

Permanent, version‑controlled, lineage‑critical records.

2. Department Libraries

Local knowledge hubs for each division.

3. Shared Knowledge Pools

Cross‑department resources and collaborative assets.

4. AI Quadrant Memory

Strategist, Everator, Muse, and Archivist store dynamic intelligence.

5. Ritual Records

Emotional and cultural knowledge preserved through ceremony.

Retrieval System

How knowledge is accessed.

1. Search Protocols

Structured queries across archives and libraries.

2. AI Quadrant Assistance

Guided retrieval through Strategist, Everator, Muse, Archivist.

3. Department Gatekeepers

Leads provide access to specialised knowledge.

4. Ritual Retrieval

Knowledge accessed through ceremony or cultural practice.

5. Continuity Access

Emergency retrieval during disruption or contingency.

Knowledge Evolution

How knowledge grows and adapts.

1. Versioning

All updates tracked with full history.

2. Review Cycles

Quarterly, annual, and canon reviews.

3. Cross‑Department Input

Shared insights strengthen knowledge quality.

4. AI Quadrant Refinement

Continuous improvement through analysis and pattern detection.

5. Ritual Renewal

Cultural and emotional knowledge refreshed through ceremony.
