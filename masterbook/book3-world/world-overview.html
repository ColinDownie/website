Realities Overview

Book IX — Realities

Introduction

Realities define the different layers of “world” within the Ross Universe. They describe how everyday life, mythic spaces, dream‑states, and cosmic planes overlap, collide, and influence one another. A reality is not just a place — it is a way of experiencing existence.

The Major Reality Layers

The Ross Universe is structured around several key reality types:

  • The Everyday World — ordinary life, work, family, and visible culture.
  • The Mythic Layer — rituals, symbols, and unseen forces shaping events.
  • The Dreamfield — inner landscapes of memory, fear, hope, and vision.
  • The Threshold Zones — liminal spaces between worlds and states.
  • The Cosmic Realms — the highest planes tied to cosmology and fate.

These layers can be experienced separately or simultaneously, depending on the character, ritual, or moment in the story.

Movement Between Realities

Movement between realities is usually triggered by:

  • Ritual — formal acts that open or close access.
  • Emotion — intense feelings that thin the boundary between layers.
  • Artefacts — objects that act as keys or anchors.
  • Places — landscapes and structures built on thresholds.
  • Cosmic Cycles — seasons and alignments that shift reality states.

Role of Clans in Reality

Different clans relate to realities in distinct ways:

  • Green — stabilise and interpret the Everyday World and Mythic Layer.
  • Orange — disrupt, explore, and express across layers.
  • Zen — hold balance between realities, especially Threshold Zones.
  • Seraphic — fracture and reforge realities during transformation events.

Notes

Future expansions may include detailed maps of each reality, case studies of crossings, and reality‑specific rituals.
