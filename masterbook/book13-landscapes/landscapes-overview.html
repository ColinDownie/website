Landscapes Overview

Book XIII — Landscapes

Introduction

Landscapes define the physical and emotional geography of the Ross Universe. They shape how characters move, where rituals unfold, and how realms feel to those who inhabit them. Every hill, glen, river, and skyline carries a quiet resonance of lineage and myth.

Main Content

This book introduces three major layers of geography:

  • Realms — large‑scale domains with distinct emotional and mythic signatures.
  • Regions — specific areas within realms, tied to clans, stories, or rituals.
  • Thresholds — liminal spaces where transitions, crossings, and transformations occur.

Together, these layers create the environmental architecture of the Ross Universe — a world that feels lived‑in, navigable, and emotionally charged.

Notes

Future expansions may include maps, elevation sketches, and realm‑to‑realm travel routes.
