Thresholds

Book XIII — Landscapes

Introduction

Thresholds are liminal landscapes — places of crossing, decision, and transformation. They mark the boundaries between realms, between emotional states, and between stages of life. To step through a threshold is to accept change.

Main Content

Key Thresholds include:

  • The Green‑Orange Gate — an archway marking the symbolic passage between calm and fire, division and unity.
  • The Mistline Pass — a fog‑shrouded route where travellers confront doubt, fear, and hidden truths.
  • The Lantern Bridge — a crossing lit by hanging lights, used in remembrance and reconciliation rituals.
  • The Echoing Tunnel — a stone passage where spoken words seem to return altered, hinting at future outcomes.
  • The Zenith Ledge — a high, narrow outcrop where pivotal choices are made in solitude.

These thresholds shape the emotional choreography of the Ross Universe — marking moments of departure, return, revelation, and renewal.

Notes
