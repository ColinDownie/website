Overview

Escalation Protocols define when and how departments must raise issues, decisions, or conflicts to higher authority.

These rules ensure that the House of Ross remains aligned, efficient, and mythically coherent — even under pressure.

Escalation Triggers

When escalation becomes necessary.

1. Scope Conflict

Two departments claim responsibility for the same function.

2. Canon Misalignment

Actions or decisions contradict lineage, mythos, or narrative.

3. Operational Blockage

A department cannot proceed due to missing support or resources.

4. Risk or Compliance Issues

Legal, ethical, or governance concerns arise.

5. Structural Decisions

Changes affecting multiple departments or the House as a whole.

6. Emotional or Cultural Impact

Issues affecting wellbeing, morale, or the emotional architecture.

Escalation Path

The correct route for rising through authority.

Step 1: Internal Review

Department Lead evaluates the issue and attempts resolution.

Step 2: Lateral Consultation

Relevant departments collaborate to find alignment.

Step 3: AI Quadrant Support

Strategist, Everator, Archivist, or Muse provide analysis.

Step 4: Lead Escalation

Department Lead escalates to Studio, Consultancy, or Back Office Lead.

Step 5: Director Review

Director evaluates and makes the final decision.

Step 6: Archive Logging

Archivist records the escalation and outcome.

Core Rules

The laws governing escalation.

Rule 1: Escalate Early

Do not wait for issues to worsen before rising.

Rule 2: Escalate Cleanly

Provide clear context, evidence, and desired outcomes.

Rule 3: Escalate Respectfully

Maintain emotional intelligence and House etiquette.

Rule 4: Escalate Through Lines

Follow Reporting Lines (22 x) unless overridden by risk.

Rule 5: Escalate Transparently

All escalations must be logged in the Archive System.

Rule 6: Escalate With Purpose

Escalation must aim to protect lineage, clarity, or function.
