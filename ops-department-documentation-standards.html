Overview

Documentation Standards define how departments record knowledge, decisions, processes, and lineage‑critical information.

These standards ensure clarity, consistency, accessibility, and mythic coherence across the House of Ross.

Document Types

The core categories of departmental documentation.

1. Operational Documents

Workflows, procedures, checklists, systems.

2. Strategic Documents

Plans, roadmaps, structural decisions.

3. Canon Documents

Mythic alignment, lineage notes, narrative coherence.

4. Cultural Documents

Wellbeing practices, emotional architecture, rituals.

5. Archive Records

Finalised decisions, lifecycle events, historical logs.

Document Structure

The required format for all documents.

1. Title Block

Clear title, department, version, date, author.

2. Purpose Statement

Why the document exists and what it protects.

3. Scope

What the document covers and what it does not.

4. Content Body

Structured sections, numbered lists, clear hierarchy.

5. Canon Notes

Mythic alignment and lineage considerations.

6. Archive Metadata

Tags, categories, version history, related documents.

Writing Standards

The rules for how documents must be written.

Rule 1: Clarity

Use precise, unambiguous language.

Rule 2: Consistency

Follow House style, tone, and formatting.

Rule 3: Accessibility

Documents must be easy to find, read, and use.

Rule 4: Canon Alignment

Ensure mythic and lineage coherence.

Rule 5: Emotional Intelligence

Muse AI supports tone, empathy, and cultural resonance.

Rule 6: Version Control

All updates must be logged and traceable.
