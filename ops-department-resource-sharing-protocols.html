Overview

Resource Sharing Protocols define how departments exchange tools, assets, time, and expertise.

This ensures that the House of Ross operates as a unified ecosystem — efficient, generous, and mythically coherent.

Resource Types

What departments can share.

1. Tools & Software

Shared platforms, creative tools, operational systems.

2. Assets

Templates, brand elements, visuals, documents, libraries.

3. Time

Cross‑department support, project assistance, reviews.

4. Talent

Specialist skills, creative expertise, technical knowledge.

Core Rules

The laws that govern resource sharing.

Rule 1: Purpose Alignment

Resources must be shared in service of the House’s mission.

Rule 2: Transparency

All shared resources must be documented and trackable.

Rule 3: Respect for Ownership

Departments retain stewardship of their core assets.

Rule 4: Fair Access

Resources must be shared equitably across the House.

Rule 5: Emotional Intelligence

Muse AI supports tone, fairness, and cultural cohesion.

Rule 6: Archive Logging

Significant exchanges must be recorded in the Archive System.

Sharing Path

The correct sequence for requesting and sharing resources.

Step 1: Request

A department submits a clear, structured request.

Step 2: Evaluation

Owning department reviews feasibility and impact.

Step 3: AI Quadrant Support

Strategist, Everator, Archivist, or Muse provide guidance.

Step 4: Approval

Department Lead or Director authorises the sharing.

Step 5: Transfer

Resources are shared with clarity and documentation.

Step 6: Review

Departments evaluate the effectiveness of the exchange.
