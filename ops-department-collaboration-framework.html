Overview

The Collaboration Framework defines how departments work together, share resources, co‑create, and support each other.

It ensures that the House of Ross operates as a unified organism — efficient, emotionally intelligent, and mythically coherent.

Collaboration Types

The core modes of working together.

1. Creative Collaboration

Joint ideation, concept development, and artistic alignment.

2. Operational Collaboration

Shared workflows, resource coordination, and process integration.

3. Strategic Collaboration

Long‑term planning, structural decisions, and cross‑department initiatives.

4. Emotional Collaboration

Support, wellbeing, and cultural alignment guided by Muse AI.

Core Rules

The laws that govern collaboration.

Rule 1: Shared Purpose

All collaborations must serve the House’s lineage and mission.

Rule 2: Defined Roles

Each department must know its responsibilities within the collaboration.

Rule 3: Transparent Communication

Updates, decisions, and changes must be shared openly.

Rule 4: Respect for Expertise

Departments defer to each other’s domain mastery.

Rule 5: Emotional Intelligence

Muse AI supports tone, empathy, and cultural cohesion.

Rule 6: Documentation

All collaborative outcomes must be logged in the Archive System.

Collaboration Path

The correct sequence for initiating and completing collaboration.

Step 1: Initiation

A department identifies a need and proposes collaboration.

Step 2: Alignment Meeting

Departments define purpose, roles, and expected outcomes.

Step 3: AI Quadrant Support

Everator, Strategist, Archivist, or Muse provide guidance.

Step 4: Execution

Departments work together using shared workflows and tools.

Step 5: Review

Departments evaluate outcomes and identify improvements.

Step 6: Archive Logging

Archivist records the collaboration and its results.
