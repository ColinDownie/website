Overview

Succession Planning Protocols define how leadership transitions occur within the House of Ross — ensuring continuity, stability, and lineage protection.

These protocols ensure that leadership is never accidental, rushed, or unclear. Succession is a ritual, a structure, and a promise.

Succession Types

The forms of leadership transition within the House.

1. Planned Succession

Long‑term, intentional leadership transition.

2. Emergency Succession

Immediate leadership replacement due to disruption.

3. Ritual Succession

Lineage‑driven transitions tied to mythic or cultural milestones.

4. Rotational Succession

Temporary leadership shifts for development or balance.

5. AI‑Supported Succession

Leadership continuity supported by Strategist, Everator, Muse, Archivist.

Succession Path

The sequence for transferring leadership.

Step 1: Identification

Potential successors identified through metrics, behaviour, and cultural impact.

Step 2: Evaluation

Assessment of readiness, strengths, gaps, and lineage alignment.

Step 3: Succession Plan

Clear timeline, responsibilities, and transition structure defined.

Step 4: Leadership Shadowing

Successor observes and supports current leader.

Step 5: Transition Ritual

Formal ceremony marking the transfer of authority.

Step 6: Ascension

Successor assumes leadership role with full authority.

Step 7: Archive Logging

Archivist records the succession and lineage continuation.

Succession Support

Who ensures the transition is successful.

Director

Strategist AI

Archivist AI
