Overview

Governance ensures that every department operates with clarity, integrity, and alignment to the Ross lineage.

These rules define boundaries, responsibilities, compliance expectations, and the standards required to maintain mythic and operational coherence.

Governance Pillars

The foundation of departmental law.

1. Purpose Integrity

Every department must operate within its defined purpose and scope.

2. Canon Alignment

All actions must align with the House’s mythic, narrative, and lineage canon.

3. Compliance

Departments must follow legal, ethical, and operational standards.

4. Transparency

Decisions, changes, and updates must be documented and accessible.

5. Accountability

Roles, responsibilities, and outcomes must be clearly assigned.

6. Evolution

Departments must adapt through periodic reviews and lifecycle stages.

Core Rules

The laws every department must follow.

Rule 1: Defined Scope

Departments must not operate outside their documented purpose.

Rule 2: Documentation

All decisions, changes, and updates must be recorded.

Rule 3: Review Cycle

Quarterly and annual reviews are mandatory.

Rule 4: Canon Protection

No department may contradict established lineage or mythos.

Rule 5: Collaboration

Departments must support each other and avoid siloing.

Rule 6: Retirement Protocol

Departments must follow lifecycle rules when closing.
