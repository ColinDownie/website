Overview

Reporting & Transparency Standards define how departments communicate progress, risks, decisions, and outcomes to the House of Ross.

These standards ensure clarity, consistency, accountability, and mythic coherence across all communication.

Report Types

The core communication formats.

1. Operational Reports

Weekly or monthly updates on workflows and output.

2. Strategic Reports

Long‑term planning, milestones, and structural decisions.

3. Creative Reports

Concept evolution, artistic direction, emotional resonance.

4. Canon Reports

Mythic alignment, lineage notes, narrative coherence.

5. Cultural Reports

Wellbeing, rituals, morale, emotional architecture.

6. Risk Reports

Emerging issues, mitigation plans, and structural impact.

Report Structure

The required format for all reports.

1. Title Block

Report name, department, date, version.

2. Summary

Clear overview of key points and outcomes.

3. Metrics

Performance data, KPIs, lineage indicators.

4. Insights

Analysis, patterns, risks, opportunities.

5. Actions

Next steps, responsibilities, timelines.

6. Canon Notes

Mythic alignment and narrative considerations.

Transparency Expectations

What departments must uphold.

1. Accuracy

Reports must be factual, complete, and verifiable.

2. Timeliness

Reports delivered on schedule without delay.

3. Clarity

Information must be easy to understand and navigate.

4. Consistency

All departments follow the same structure and tone.

5. Accessibility

Reports stored in the Archive and easy to retrieve.

6. Canon Alignment

Communication must honour lineage and mythic coherence.
