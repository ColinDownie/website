Overview

Leadership Development Protocols define how the House of Ross identifies, trains, and elevates future leaders across all departments.

These protocols ensure that leadership is consistent, emotionally intelligent, operationally strong, and mythically aligned.

Leadership Competencies

The qualities required of a House of Ross leader.

1. Operational Mastery

Deep understanding of systems, workflows, and execution.

2. Creative Vision

Ability to ideate, inspire, and shape artistic direction.

3. Strategic Thinking

Long‑term planning, structural awareness, future‑proofing.

4. Canon Stewardship

Protecting lineage, mythos, and narrative coherence.

5. Emotional Intelligence

Empathy, communication, cultural sensitivity, wellbeing.

6. Ritual Leadership

Ability to guide ceremonies, milestones, and House culture.

Leadership Path

The sequence for becoming a leader in the House.

Step 1: Identification

Potential leaders identified through metrics, behaviour, and cultural impact.

Step 2: Assessment

Evaluation of strengths, gaps, and lineage responsibilities.

Step 3: Leadership Training Plan

Custom curriculum covering operational, creative, strategic, and canon skills.

Step 4: Mentorship

Guidance from Director, Leads, and AI Quadrant.

Step 5: Leadership Trials

Projects, rituals, and responsibilities that test readiness.

Step 6: Ascension

Formal elevation to leadership role with ceremony.

Step 7: Archive Logging

Archivist records the ascension and leadership lineage.
